GIVING IS AN ACT OF WORSHIP

YOUR GENEROSITY MAKES A DIFFERENCE

Sadqa, a core Islamic principle, embodies generosity and compassion toward the less fortunate. It signifies voluntary acts of charity, given without expecting anything in return. Prophet Muhammad's (peace be upon him) teachings underscore the importance of Sadqa, urging believers to assist those in need. It encompasses various forms of giving, from monetary donations to providing basic necessities. Sincere intentions and humility are vital aspects, as Sadqa is meant to be selfless and solely for the sake of pleasing Allah. Through Sadqa, Muslims aim to purify their wealth, foster empathy, and fulfill their duty to help those in need.

  • Sadqa is an act of voluntary charity in Islam, where one gives to those in need.
  • There are different types of Sadqa, including Zakat and Sadaqah al-Fitr.
  • Giving Sadqa is highly encouraged in Islam.
  • Sadqa can be given at any time and in any amount
  • Sadqa have a significant imapact
